# Platform Playbooks

Use these platform notes as baseline heuristics. Prefer account-level analytics over generic timing advice whenever real data exists.

## Use LinkedIn for B2B Authority

- Use for thought leadership, professional networking, recruiting, and professional storytelling.
- Target professionals, decision-makers, operators, and job seekers.
- Start with 3-5 posts per week.
- Test Tuesday-Thursday around 7-8am, 12pm, and 5-6pm as baseline windows.
- Prioritize personal stories with business lessons, contrarian takes, behind-the-scenes lessons, data-backed insights, carousels, and discussion polls.
- Avoid corporate filler, over-promotion, generic motivation, and links in the main post body.
- Put the first line to work before the "see more" break.
- Use line breaks aggressively for readability.
- Keep most text posts in the rough range of 1,200-1,500 characters unless brevity is the point.
- Put links in comments when reach matters more than direct click-through efficiency.
- Tag sparingly and only when the mention is genuinely relevant.

## Use Twitter/X for Community and Real-Time Attention

- Use for tech, media, niche communities, commentary, and fast feedback loops.
- Target highly online, news-aware, and conversation-driven audiences.
- Start with 3-10 posts per day, including replies.
- Use audience testing rather than fixed posting times.
- Prioritize hot takes, tutorial threads, founder notes, real-time commentary, humor that fits the brand, and active replies.
- Avoid pure self-promotion, weak thread openings, ignoring mentions, and fully automated presence.
- Keep many standalone posts under 100 characters when possible.
- Open every thread with a hook and a clear promise.
- Prefer quote posts with added insight over passive reposting.
- Use visuals when they materially improve stop rate or understanding.

## Use Instagram for Visual Education and Lifestyle Signaling

- Use for visual brands, lifestyle positioning, e-commerce, and creator-led education.
- Target visual-first consumers, usually 18-44.
- Start with 1-2 feed posts per day and 3-10 stories per day.
- Test 11am-1pm and 7-9pm as baseline windows.
- Prioritize carousels, reels, behind-the-scenes stories, user-generated content, and interactive story features.
- Avoid low-quality visuals, text-heavy creative, story neglect, and a feed made entirely of promotions.
- Treat reels as the main discovery format.
- Make the first reel frame and first caption line do real hook work.
- Build educational carousels with one clear point per slide.
- Use polls, questions, links, and stickers to keep stories active.

## Use TikTok for Native Short-Form Discovery

- Use for awareness, entertainment-led education, and trend participation.
- Target younger and entertainment-oriented audiences, often 16-34.
- Start with 1-4 posts per day.
- Test 7-9am, 12-3pm, and 7-11pm as baseline windows.
- Prioritize native-feeling clips, trends, POV formats, day-in-the-life content, comment-response videos, stitches, and duets.
- Avoid over-produced edits, obvious repurposed horizontal video, hard selling, and trend blindness.
- Hook within the first 1-2 seconds.
- Start under 30 seconds unless the concept clearly earns more time.
- Keep all content vertical and mobile-native.
- Use trending sounds only when they help the idea.

## Use Facebook for Community and Mature Audiences

- Use for communities, local businesses, groups, older demographics, and discussion-led distribution.
- Target community-oriented audiences, often 25-55+.
- Start with 1-2 posts per day.
- Test 1-4pm on weekdays as a baseline.
- Prioritize native video, live video, local stories, group discussions, and question-led posts.
- Avoid pure promotion, ignored comments, heavy outbound linking, and lazy cross-posting from other platforms.
- Treat groups as a separate strategy, not just another feed.
- Favor conversation prompts over polished announcements.

## Adapt to Platform Algorithms

### Adapt for LinkedIn

- Optimize for strong first-hour engagement.
- Prefer comments over passive reactions.
- Increase dwell time with readable structure and curiosity.
- Prefer document posts when the idea is teachable step by step.

### Adapt for Twitter/X

- Reply often and add value in public.
- Prefer threads when the idea needs sequential explanation.
- Use images or short video when they sharpen the point.

### Adapt for Instagram

- Optimize for saves and shares, not only likes.
- Keep stories active to stay top-of-feed.
- Stay consistent even when production quality varies.

### Adapt for TikTok

- Reward speed, authenticity, and trend fluency.
- Treat retention as the main creative constraint.

### Adapt for Facebook

- Prioritize comments and community participation.
- Keep the conversation going after publishing.
