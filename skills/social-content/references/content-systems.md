# Content Systems

## Build Content Pillars

Build 3-5 recurring pillars that connect audience interest to business value.

Use this founder-oriented mix as a starting point, then adapt it:

| Pillar | Share of content | Typical topics |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Industry insights | 30% | Trends, market shifts, predictions, observations |
| Behind the scenes | 25% | Process, lessons learned, building in public |
| Educational | 25% | How-tos, frameworks, checklists, mistakes |
| Personal | 15% | Values, stories, opinions, experiences |
| Promotional | 5% | Offers, launches, product updates |

Use these questions to define each pillar:

- What unique perspective can the brand defend?
- What questions does the audience ask repeatedly?
- What has performed well before?
- What can the team create consistently?
- What supports the actual business goal?

## Repurpose One Asset into Many

Turn one strong source asset into multiple native outputs.

### Repurpose a Blog Post

| Source | Platform | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Blog post | LinkedIn | Insight-led post |
| Blog post | LinkedIn | Carousel of key points |
| Blog post | Twitter/X | Thread of takeaways |
| Blog post | Twitter/X | One hot-take post |
| Blog post | Instagram | Carousel |
| Blog post | Instagram | Reel summary |

### Repurpose a Podcast or Video

| Source | Platform | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Interview | LinkedIn | Quote + insight |
| Interview | Twitter/X | Thread of quotes or lessons |
| Interview | Instagram | Reel clip |
| Interview | TikTok | Short native clip |
| Interview | YouTube Shorts | Best-moment short |

### Follow the Repurposing Workflow

1. Create one pillar asset.
2. Extract 3-5 key insights.
3. Match each insight to a platform-native format.
4. Spread distribution across the week.
5. Reuse evergreen winners with updated framing.

## Build a Content Calendar

Use this weekly pattern as a default structure:

| Day | LinkedIn | Twitter/X | Instagram |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Industry insight | Thread | Carousel |
| Tuesday | Behind the scenes | Engagement post | Story |
| Wednesday | Educational | Tips post | Reel |
| Thursday | Story post | Thread | Educational carousel |
| Friday | Hot take | Engagement post | Story |
| Saturday | Optional | Curated repost or reply-first day | User or community content |
| Sunday | Optional | Personal reflection | Behind-the-scenes story |

Use this monthly rhythm when the user asks for variety:

- Week 1: Announce, launch, or frame a point of view.
- Week 2: Teach something in depth.
- Week 3: Focus on community and conversation.
- Week 4: Share stories, process, or behind-the-scenes proof.

## Batch Production

Use a 2-3 hour weekly batching block when the user wants a sustainable system:

1. Review pillar topics.
2. Draft 5 LinkedIn posts.
3. Draft 3 X threads plus short daily post ideas.
4. Outline carousel and reel ideas.
5. Schedule core content.
6. Leave room for live commentary and replies.

## Run an Engagement Routine

Treat engagement as part of distribution, not as cleanup after publishing.

Use this daily baseline:

- Reply to comments on active posts.
- Leave 5-10 substantive comments on target accounts.
- Repost or quote content with added insight.
- Start 2-3 direct conversations when appropriate.

Use quality-comment rules:

- Add a new insight.
- Share a relevant experience.
- Ask a follow-up question.
- Disagree respectfully when disagreement helps the conversation.

Use relationship-building rules:

- Track 20-50 relevant accounts.
- Engage consistently before asking for anything.
- Share others' work with credit.
- Use collaboration after repeated public interaction.

Use negative-comment rules:

- Stay calm.
- Avoid defensive replies.
- Move real criticism offline when needed.
- Block or mute trolls instead of feeding them.

## Track the Right Metrics

### Track Awareness

- Impressions
- Reach
- Follower growth rate

### Track Engagement

- Engagement rate
- Comments
- Shares or reposts
- Saves on Instagram

### Track Conversion

- Link clicks
- Profile visits
- DMs
- Leads or attributed conversions

### Review Weekly

- Top 3 posts and why they worked
- Bottom 3 posts and what failed
- Follower trend
- Engagement-rate trend
- Best posting windows from actual data
- Content-pillar performance

## Optimize Based on Signals

If engagement is low:

- Test stronger hooks.
- Change posting windows.
- Change the format.
- Increase proactive engagement.
- Re-check audience-content fit.

If reach declines:

- Remove unnecessary outbound links from the main body.
- Test a modest frequency increase.
- Publish more native visual content.
- Increase comment activity on and off your own posts.

## Use Situation-Based Ideas

When the brand is early:

- Document the journey.
- Share lessons learned.
- Curate with commentary.
- Ask the audience questions.

When the brand is established:

- Publish original data and frameworks.
- Share stronger opinions.
- Use customer proof.
- Collaborate with peers.

When the brand is stuck:

- Reframe past winners.
- Ask the audience what they need.
- React to industry news.
- Share a failure and lesson.
- Interview someone and distribute the takeaways.

## Schedule Intentionally

Schedule these:

- Core planned posts
- Threads
- Carousels
- Evergreen content

Post live when immediacy matters:

- News reactions
- Real-time event commentary
- Fast community conversations
- Trend participation

Keep 1-2 weeks of scheduled content, then review it weekly for relevance.
