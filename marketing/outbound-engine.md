# Outbound Engine

Last updated: 2026-03-17

## Goal

Generate the fastest early pipeline by targeting owner-led NC Triad contractors with visible website conversion problems and offering a short website teardown / lead conversion audit.

## Weekly Motion

- research 30 qualified leads
- contact 20-25 leads
- send audits selectively to the highest-fit responders
- follow up all open threads
- book 3-5 calls per month pace

## Prospect Qualification Criteria

A prospect is outbound-worthy if several of these are visible:

- outdated design
- poor mobile experience
- weak CTA
- no specific service pages
- buried or unclear contact path
- weak or missing reviews
- obvious credibility gap
- generic messaging
- site looks weaker than the business likely is

## Prospect Scoring Rubric

| Factor | Weight | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| local fit | 20 | Winston-Salem and Greensboro first |
| trade fit | 20 | masonry/hardscaping, deck/fence, concrete |
| site weakness severity | 20 | visible conversion blockers |
| business is active | 15 | fresh reviews, recent posts, active jobs, current phone |
| ease of personalization | 10 | one obvious website issue and clear local angle |
| likely budget fit | 15 | established enough to invest in a site |

Score out of 100. Contact only leads above 65 unless there is a strong relationship angle.

## Research Fields

- company name
- website
- city
- trade
- phone
- email or contact path
- latest review date
- key website problem
- screenshot path or audit notes
- score

## Core Hook

"3 fixes stopping your site from turning searches into calls."

## 4-Touch Cold Email Sequence

### Email 1: observation + offer

Subject: one quick fix

Hi {{first_name}},

I was looking at {{company_name}} and noticed a few easy website issues that could be costing you calls, especially on mobile.

The biggest one: {{specific_issue}}.

I put together a short teardown with 3 fixes that would make the site easier for local prospects to trust and contact.

Want me to send it over?

Alex

### Email 2: second issue

Subject: another thing I noticed

Hi {{first_name}},

One more thing I noticed on the site: {{second_specific_issue}}.

It is small, but it makes it harder for someone to quickly understand what you do and how to reach you.

If helpful, I can send the short teardown with the 3 highest-priority fixes.

Alex

### Email 3: teardown and credibility angle

Subject: short teardown

Hi {{first_name}},

I work with contractor websites and usually see the same pattern:

- the work looks strong
- the website looks weaker than the business
- local prospects hesitate or bounce before calling

I can send a quick teardown with screenshots and the fixes I would make first.

Worth sending?

Alex

### Email 4: polite closeout

Subject: close this out?

Hi {{first_name}},

I will close this out after this note.

If you ever want the quick teardown of the site and the 3 fixes I would start with, reply with "send it" and I will put it together.

Alex

### DM 2: follow-up after email

Sent you a note with a quick website observation. If it is easier here, I can just send the short teardown directly in LinkedIn.

## Call Opener

When a lead replies or books:

1. confirm the problem they are feeling
2. show that you understand the trade and local context
3. walk through the biggest conversion blockers
4. translate the blockers into missed trust or missed calls
5. move into what a stronger site would actually do for them

## Stop Rules

- stop after the 4th touch if there is no response
- stop immediately on opt-out
- stop if the business is clearly outside the ICP
- move interested but bad-timing leads into nurture
