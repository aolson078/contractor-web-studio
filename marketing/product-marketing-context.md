# Product Marketing Context

Last updated: 2026-03-17

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Custom contractor websites for NC Triad trades businesses that do great work but look weaker online than they are in real life.

**What it does:** Contractor Web Studio designs and builds custom websites for owner-led trades businesses that need a stronger digital presence, better trust signals, and clearer conversion paths. The service is designed for contractors who rely heavily on referrals but lose opportunities because their website, mobile experience, or lead capture system undercuts their real-world reputation.

**Category:** Contractor website design / contractor web studio / local web design for trades

**Type:** Productized local service business

**Business model:** Project-based website builds with optional recurring maintenance and local growth add-ons

## Target Audience

**Primary ICP:**

- owner-led contractors in the NC Triad
- roughly 2-20 employees
- strong workmanship, weak digital presence
- outdated site, Facebook-only presence, or low-trust website
- limited service page depth and no strong lead capture system

**Primary cities:** Winston-Salem, Greensboro

**Secondary city:** High Point

**Primary trades:** masonry/hardscaping, deck/fence

**Secondary trade:** concrete

## Anti-ICP

- large regional firms with internal marketing teams
- contractors already running strong SEO and lead systems
- bargain-basement buyers who want the cheapest possible template site
- clients who need broad brand consulting before they need a website that converts
- enterprise workflow buyers in V1

## Problems and Pain Points

**Core problem:** Their workmanship and reputation are stronger than their digital presence, so local prospects trust competitors faster than they trust them.

**Visible symptoms:**

- outdated design
- poor mobile usability
- weak or buried contact path
- no clear service structure
- no good review/reputation handoff
- generic messaging
- weak trust indicators
- no local landing page depth

**Business cost:**

- fewer calls from local search
- weaker close rate on referral-driven leads who look them up online
- lower perceived credibility
- over-reliance on word of mouth

**Emotional tension:**

- frustration that weaker operators look more established online
- skepticism about paying for a website
- concern about getting locked into tools or jargon-heavy agencies

## Customer Language

**How they describe the problem:**

- "People are finding us, but they do not call."
- "Our work looks better than our website."
- "We rely on referrals because our online presence does not help us."
- "Competitors look more legit online even when they are not better."

**Words to use:**

- local
- contractor-specific
- custom
- mobile-friendly
- trust
- calls
- quotes
- clean
- professional
- easier to hire

**Words to avoid:**

- synergy
- leverage
- funnel hacking
- disruption
- growth ninja
- jargon-heavy agency language

## Differentiation

- focused on contractors, not generic small business sites
- local-first positioning for NC Triad trades
- custom builds rather than generic template-first work
- trust and conversion framing, not just visual polish
- lean process with direct access to the builder
- proof discipline and clear handoff into calls

## Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "I get enough work from referrals." | Referrals still look you up online. The site helps close the leads you already earn and captures local demand you miss today. |
| "I do not need anything fancy." | The goal is not fancy. The goal is clear services, stronger trust, and easier calls and quote requests. |
| "I can just use Wix or WordPress." | Builders are fine until they create slow, generic pages that do not match the trade, local market, or conversion job. |
| "I do not have time for a big process." | The offer is productized, narrow, and built to move fast with simple inputs and direct communication. |

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**

- local freelance web designers
- generalist small business web agencies
- niche contractor marketing shops

**Secondary competitors:**

- Wix, Squarespace, WordPress theme shops
- Facebook-only presence
- "someone's nephew built the site"

**Indirect competitors:**

- pure referral-only growth
- no-site Google Business Profile plus phone number

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** direct, practical, local, no-fluff

**Style:** conversational, credible, plain English

**Personality:** grounded, contractor-aware, sharp, honest

## Proof Policy

Only use:

- approved testimonials already present in the repo
- real portfolio screenshots
- real project descriptions
- grounded case studies

Never use:

- invented ROI claims
- fake logos
- synthetic client wins
- inflated results language that cannot be defended

- approved testimonial set in the repo
